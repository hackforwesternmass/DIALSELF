RISE-Resources Invested in Student Excellence
Communities and Schools Working Together
  • The RISE initiative is a school-community partnership that seeks to enhance attachment to school amongst Middle School and/or High School students.  RISE accomplishes this goal by providing meaningful opportunities for students to be involved in their school community, and for community members to be involved at the Middle and/ High School in their community.
11
RISE modeled after 'Communities in Schools'
  • Communities in Schools (CIS) is the largest and most successful organization in the country focused on integrating schools and communities.  Begun in 1977, CIS is now serving over 3,000 school sites in 28 states.  More than 2 million students annually benefit from CIS programs.


  • CIS programs help students successfully learn, stay in school, and better prepare for life.  A local CIS accomplishes these goals by bringing community resources into schools, and by making schools a resource to the community.
12
Among CIS tracked students:
  • 79% improved attendance
  • 81% had fewer incidents of discipline
  • 88% improved academic performance
  • 85% were promoted to the next grade
  • 86% of eligible seniors graduated
  • 2% dropped out, lower than the national average of 4 percent and lower than the estimated 6-10 percent dropout rate for student populations similar to those served by CIS.
13
How RISE works:
  •  RISE places a “Resource Coordinator” in the Middle School, who works with students, teachers, administrators, and parents to identify needs for enhanced programming and services
  • The RISE Coordinator then works with local businesspeople, service agencies, and volunteers to bring services and programs to the school.
  • Ultimately, RISE will enable the Middle School to become a “full service community school”, where programs and services are available before, during, and after school, as well as on weekends and during school vacations.
14
 RISE-Coordinated Services:
  • Recruiting and training volunteers
  • Identifying college students who seek internships in the youth development field and bringing those students into the Middle School to provide enrichment activities
  • Collaborating with Franklin Medical Center to develop health and wellness programs
  • Coordinating with community agencies to offer services to Middle School students (e.g. Big Brothers Big Sisters, Community Action!, The Franklin Hampshire Career Center, The Interfaith Council, The Youth Commission).
  • Involving the business community in career initiatives
  • Working with local arts-based initiatives to bring arts/music to the school
  • Responding to any innovative, creative opportunities dreamed up by students, parents, teachers, or community members!
